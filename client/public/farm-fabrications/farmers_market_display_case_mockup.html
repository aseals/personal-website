Tabletop cooled display case for produce

DIY build, 3D-printed frame brackets + off-the-shelf electronics. Designed for leafy greens and post-harvest produce at a farmers market stall.

Component callouts

1
Tinted polycarbonate panels
3mm UV-filtering sheets, removable for cleaning. Customer sees the goods; greens don't get cooked by direct sun.
2
Peltier cooler + heatsink fan
TEC1-12706 module, 60W, 12V. Cold plate inside, hot heatsink + 80mm fan vented out the back. Drops interior 10–15°F below ambient.
3
Ultrasonic mister
5V piezo fogger module sitting in a small reservoir. Tiny tube delivers fog from the ceiling. Maintains 85–90% relative humidity so greens don't transpire dry.
4
Electronics + battery bay
Lower cabinet houses 12V 20Ah LiFePO4 battery, buck converter (12V → 5V), ESP32 controller, and the DHT22 temp/humidity sensor breakout. Front LCD shows current readings.
5
Solar panel (optional)
20W panel laminated to the roof. Charges through an MPPT controller. With a sunny market day, the battery tops up faster than it drains.
6
3D-printed corner brackets
PETG corner joints connect aluminum extrusion (2020 profile) or wood dowels into a rigid frame. Roughly 8 printable parts total.

Open design questions to discuss

  • Front access: hinged side-opening door (drawn here) versus a top-lifting lid. Side door is easier for customers; lid keeps cold air in better.
  • Misting frequency: continuous fog versus 30 sec on / 5 min off cycle. Less water, less risk of soaking the produce.
  • Drainage: small catch tray at the bottom with a drain plug, in case misting condenses.
  • Insulation: 1/2 in foam board behind the wood base panels would extend runtime significantly.
  • Tier count: shown as 2 shelves. Could be 1 (more headroom) or 3 (more capacity, less air circulation).
